University Projects

Residential Energy Management System with Integrated Payment Card

The system I developed is an innovative mechanism that regulates the flow of energy to a household based on a smart meter. This meter allows energy to pass only when payments are up to date. Economic management is facilitated through a special card that is recharged at a designated station. This card is also used at another station to make payments for the energy consumed, providing an efficient and secure system for controlling and managing household electricity.

Voice-Controlled Car Project using Raspberry Pi Signal Processing

The project involved creating a car that could be operated through voice commands. Voice signals were processed using a Raspberry Pi to enable movement commands such as forward, backward, left, and right. This innovative approach utilized signal processing technology to enable hands-free control of the vehicle, enhancing its usability and accessibility.

Analog Control Implementation for Distance-based Car Braking

This project focused on developing a car capable of braking at a predetermined distance using analog control techniques. It involved the implementation of an analog PID (Proportional-Integral-Derivative) control system. By integrating this system, the car could autonomously regulate its braking mechanism, ensuring a consistent and reliable braking distance. This innovative use of analog control technology aimed to enhance safety and precision in vehicle operations.
